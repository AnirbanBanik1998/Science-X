About Us

Small drops of water together can form an ocean, similarly, small ideas on proper development can bring in a revolution . We team Aavishkar are here providing a platform for all students with creative ideas , and turn them into a wildfire by setting it ablaze through models.School and college students can submit their ideas by making them into a model by forming teams with maximum of 4 members.

The models submitted can win a chance to be displayed in NIT Durgapur during the Technical fest Aavishkar 2.0 for 3 days of the event. It will put up to display for professors, students and various other people to check on it. Best model on display may even get a chance to be displayed in museum.

A famous quote by Albert Einstein goes like this, "Imagination is more important than knowledge. Knowledge is limited. Imagination encircles the world." So don’t miss this once in a year opportunity that is awaiting for your ideas which might inspire many people when put into use.

Rules

To participate please refer to the rules and guidelines mentioned below:

    School Students:
  1. Each team must have a mentor and maximum of 2 teams from each school.
  2. The prototype of the model i.e the idea based on which model is to be made must be submitted on or before 9th October.
  3. Models can be presented on any topic or an idea.
  4. Along with submission of model and prototype, a description on the idea and it’s uses must be submitted.

    Undergraduate Students:
  1. Each team can have maximum of 4 participants in it.
  2. The prototype of the model i.e the idea based on which model is to be made must be submitted on or before 9th October.
  3. Ideas must be presented on one of the following domains:
  • Health & medicare
  • Transport & Communication
  • Agriculture
  • Wastage management and utilisation
  • Renewable energy
  • Cyber security
  • Smart city
  • Disaster management
  • Other topics but the domain of their model must be mentioned along with it.

    Models from Departments:
  1. M.tech and PhD scholars can submit their entries under this category.
  2. Maximum of 4 participants in a team.
  3. Models can be presented on any domain, but detailed description on the idea of the model, it’s uses and it’s classification i.e under which domain does the model fall into must be submitted along with the model.
  4. The prototype of the model i.e the idea based on which model is to be made must be submitted on or before 9th October.
